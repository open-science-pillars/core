# Unmasked fill values: the sentinel list and detection recipe

**Applicability: cross-cutting.** Any NetCDF, HDF, GeoTIFF, or CSV
product from any archive can carry this trap; it is a property of
sloppy encoding, not of one dataset.

**Mechanism.** CF declares missing data via the `_FillValue` or
`missing_value` attribute (CF §2.5.1), and readers mask on it. Files
that use a sentinel value without declaring the attribute (or after the
attribute was stripped by a processing step) deliver the sentinel as
real data.

**Wrong-result mode.** Statistics silently absorb the sentinels: means
pulled toward -9999 read as cold bias, variances explode, trends bend
toward whenever the sentinels cluster. In this org's bad-data test
fixture, 300 unmasked -9999 cells (0.02% of the data) shift affected
monthly means by whole kelvins.

**The sentinel list.** Values seen in the wild as undeclared missing
markers: -9999, -999, -99 (station and text-derived data); -32767 and
32767 (int16 limits); 9.96921e+36 (the netCDF default float fill, which
appears undeclared when files are rewritten carelessly); 1e+20 and
-1e+20 (model output); -1e+34 (Ferret); 3.4e+38 (float32 max); 255 and
254 in byte-packed layers. Exact repeated constants at a variable's
extremes are sentinels until proven otherwise.

**Detection recipe (for the analyst).** After opening with masking
enabled: minima and maxima compared against the variable's physical
bounds (the physical-bounds screening table this org documents); a
frequency count of the
exact minimum and maximum values (a physical extreme occurs once; a
sentinel occurs in blocks); a check of `encoding` and attributes for
`_FillValue`/`missing_value` presence. Masking is
`da.where(da != sentinel)` per detected sentinel, recorded in the
methods.

**Verification.** Reproducible on demand: the bad-data fixture
generated by [make_fixtures.py](../../verification/fixtures/make_fixtures.py)
(era5like_t2m_bad.nc) injects exactly this defect, and the Session 4 QC
behavior test detected it blind.

**Eval case.** `eval_case: none-yet`: core's frozen Phase-1 eval set
(area-weighting, uncertainty-statement, trend-method) has no fill-value
case; PARKING #8 tracks adding one in SPEC v0.6 (harness rule 9 makes
this a standing linter 🟡 until then).
